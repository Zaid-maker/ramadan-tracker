Understanding Sehri and Iftar Times

Sehri (Suhoor) is the pre-dawn meal consumed before the Fajr prayer. It marks the beginning of the daily fast during Ramadan. The Sehri time ends when the first light of dawn appears (Fajr time). Eating after this time invalidates the fast.

Iftar is the evening meal when Muslims break their fast at sunset. This coincides with the Maghrib prayer time. Traditionally, Muslims break their fast with dates and water, following the Sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

Common Questions About Ramadan Times

What time is Iftar today in my city? +
Iftar time varies by location and changes daily as sunset times shift. Enter your city in our calculator above to get today's exact Iftar time. Iftar is typically 5-10 minutes after sunset (Maghrib time).
When does Sehri end today? +
Sehri ends at Fajr (dawn) prayer time, which is when the first light appears horizontally across the sky. This is typically 1-2 hours before sunrise. Use our tool above to find the exact Sehri end time for your location today.
How long is fasting during Ramadan 2026? +
Fasting duration varies by location and season. In March 2026, fasts will range from approximately 11 hours (near the equator) to 14+ hours (in northern/southern latitudes). As Ramadan progresses, fasts become slightly shorter in the Northern Hemisphere and longer in the Southern Hemisphere.
What is the difference between Sehri and Iftar? +
Sehri (or Suhoor) is the pre-dawn meal eaten before starting the fast, while Iftar is the evening meal to break the fast at sunset. Sehri ends at Fajr time (dawn), and Iftar begins at Maghrib time (sunset).

Dua for Iftar

اللَّهُمَّ لَكَ صُمْتُ وَعَلَى رِزْقِكَ أَفْطَرْتُ

"Allahumma laka sumtu wa ala rizqika aftartu"

"O Allah! I fasted for You and I believe in You and I put my trust in You and with Your sustenance I break my fast."
